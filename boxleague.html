Padel Club Box League Guidelines

1. Overview

The Box League is a friendly, competitive doubles league designed to give players regular matches and encourage variety in opponents and partners. Leagues are split into Men's, Ladies', and Mixed categories. Players are grouped into "boxes," and matches are played over a 4-month round.

2. Format

  • The league is doubles only.
  • Players are grouped by ability and category.
    View ability level descriptions
    1. Newcomer – First time playing, learning basic rules and how to hit the ball.
    2. Beginner – Can rally a little, but struggles with control, positioning, and timing.
    3. Improver – More consistent shots, starting to understand walls and basic tactics.
    4. Lower Intermediate – Can sustain rallies, control pace better, and use walls more effectively.
    5. Intermediate – Good shot control, understands positioning, and can start dictating play.
    6. Upper Intermediate – Confident play, good use of lobs, angles, and teamwork.
    7. Advanced – Strong tactical awareness, consistent under pressure, and adapts well to opponents.
    8. Competitive – High-level player, effective in attack and defense, plays structured points.
    9. Elite – Tournament-level, excels in all aspects of the game with precision and strategy.
  • Each round lasts 4 months.
  • Each pair plays every other pair in their box.
  • Top pairs may be promoted, bottom pairs relegated.

3. Box League Points

Match Format

  • Matches consist of 3 sets.
  • If the score is 1-1 after two sets, a 10-point match tiebreak is played instead of a full third set.
  • The match tiebreak is won by the first team to reach 10 points with at least a 2-point lead.

Scoring

  • 2–0 win: 5–1
  • 2–1 win: 4–2
  • Friendly walkover: 4–0

4. Match Scheduling

  • Use the WhatsApp group(s) to coordinate.
  • Suggest multiple time options and provide at least 7 days' notice.
  • Contact the league admin if opponents are unresponsive.

4a. Friendly Walkovers

When is a walkover awarded?

  • Opponent doesn't show up without prior notice
  • Last-minute cancellation with no option to reschedule
  • No replies to match messages for 7+ days
  • Player withdraws without informing the coordinator

How to request a walkover:

  1. Take screenshots of WhatsApp communication
  2. Contact the League Coordinator
  3. The coordinator will review the situation and decide fairly
Walkovers are a last resort – we encourage flexibility, good communication, and community spirit wherever possible.

5. Promotion and Relegation

  • Top 1–2 pairs may be promoted
  • Bottom 1–2 pairs may be relegated

Tiebreakers:

  1. Head-to-head result
  2. Set difference
  3. Total games won

6. Player Commitment

  • Join only if committed to playing during the round
  • Notify the coordinator if unavailable
  • Pairs with zero matches and no contact may be removed

7. Substitutes and Absences

  • Subs allowed only with admin approval
  • Subs must be similar level and gender
  • If a pair withdraws, walkovers may be awarded

8. Results Submission

  • All results must be submitted via the PadelPals app by one of the players.
  • Include the following details:
    • Set scores (e.g., 6–3, 4–6, 10–7)
    • Final result (2–0, 2–1, or walkover)
    • Date played
  • If the match reaches 1 set all, a 10-point match tiebreak should be played in place of a third set.
  • For walkovers, select the appropriate option and provide a short note in the app.
  • Matches not played or submitted by the deadline will not count unless awarded as walkovers by the coordinator.

9. Fair Play

All matches should be played in good spirit. Respect your opponents and the rules. Disputes should be resolved in good faith – the league coordinator's decision is final.
